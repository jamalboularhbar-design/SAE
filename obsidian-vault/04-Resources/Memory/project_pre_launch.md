**Why:** This is the operating doc the venture's entire launch is sequenced against. Every file in `pre-launch/` exists because the playbook called for it.

**Plan boundaries:**
- Launch window: June 2026 in New York and San Francisco
- Today's date as of the audit work: 2026-06-21/22
- Founder voice: *"I'm not selling software. I'm selling autonomous capacity — the invisible layer where work gets done."*
- Audience priority: B2B (operators, founders, ops leaders) first, B2C second

**The week-by-week plan** (as written in `pre-launch/playbook.md`):

| Week | Dates | Job |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | May 5–11 | Foundation: positioning, ICP, LinkedIn profile, banner |
| 2 | May 12–18 | Content + outreach infrastructure: 5 anchor LinkedIn posts, target list (30 prospects), 3-touch outreach sequence, vision one-pager |
| 3 | May 19–25 | Meetings + proof assets: outreach launched, pitch deck or Loom, founding-customer offer sheet, 10–15 meetings booked |
| 4 | May 26 – Jun 1 | Travel readiness: NY/SF logistics, press kit, follow-up sequence pre-loaded, final posts published |
| Trip | June 2026 | Three signed founding customers + 5 strong leads is the success bar |

**Operating principles (his own words):**
1. One thesis, repeated. Same story everywhere.
2. Show the two businesses as proof — Riad&Routes and ArtKech are the case studies.
3. Cinema-grade taste, not LinkedIn-bro energy. No 🚀, no "Excited to announce", no listicles.
4. Founding cohort is closed and small. Cap at 10 — scarcity isn't a tactic, it's the truth.
5. Don't pre-announce the trip publicly. Tell prospects directly.
