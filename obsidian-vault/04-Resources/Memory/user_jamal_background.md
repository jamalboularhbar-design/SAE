The arc, in order:

- **1995–1997, Marrakech.** Diploma in Computing & Management at a moment when most of Morocco hadn't met a computer. Windows 95 in a country still running on telephone lines.
- **1998.** Online. One of the earliest people in Morocco actually on the web. Email, forums, the whole posture of the internet learned at street level when there was no instruction manual.
- **The next 25 years.** Different career on the surface — VIP tour guide, photographer, designer, luxury travel curator. On set with *Astérix & Obélix : Mission Cléopâtre* and *Alexander* among others. Watched how a 200-person production runs without anyone shouting. Languages, history, art, cinema, the texture of how craft is actually made.
- **Underneath.** The tech kept compounding quietly. Patient.
- **Now.** Runs Riad&Routes and ArtKech through Autopilots he built. The dormant tech background isn't dormant anymore.

This is not biography — it is the venture's moat. The pitch is "a quarter-century of curating impeccable human experiences, encoded into systems that run themselves." Most AI founders have one of those backgrounds. Almost none have both.

Use this when:
- Drafting his copy (LinkedIn, posts, deck, founder block on /product)
- Choosing between thesis-led vs feature-led framing — always lead with the arc to skeptical premium buyers
- Pricing decisions — this background justifies cohort pricing, not commodity SaaS pricing
- Brand decisions — the Marrakech specificity is an asset, but only against premium NY/SF rooms. In the local Moroccan market it's table stakes.

**Why:** The audit-and-synthesis work in this project repeatedly concluded that this arc is the *only* unattackable differentiator. Notion + ChatGPT can copy the playbook product. They can't copy 25 years of taste. When in doubt, lead with the founder.

**How to apply:** Any draft where the founder is invisible is off-thesis. He even keeps a specific closing line for hard decisions:
> *"I spent twenty-five years curating the kind of experiences that can't be templated. Now I'm building the systems that run them while the humans do what only humans should do."*
